Nicole's Story

Did you always know what you wanted to do?

I left school with good A-Levels but I didn’t know what I wanted to do after. I’ve always been creative and loved making things, but not necessarily in the artistic way, I like building things; learning how and why they work. At the same time my interest in technology has always been there, ever since I first used a computer in primary school. This was in the days of Microsoft’s Encarta and I loved it, I couldn’t wait to get to school if I knew we were going into the computer room.

What happened between college and your decision to get into tech?

Life happened, I got married and had a child and I’ve been working in offices since I left college, in sales and admin but three years ago I had a daughter and decided it was time for change. I want to be inspiring to her, you know?

What has your journey into a STEM career been like?

I’m a student at the Open University, majoring in Computing and IT Software and I also spend time learning to code. A friend suggested that I start with Python, which is what I did.

What do you hope to achieve in STEM?

When I feel ready, I hope to be able to join a software company as a developer. I’d love to work somewhere that feels like a start-up so I can feel the difference I make. But even if that path never works out for me, at least I know how to code and I can keep practicing on my Raspberry Pi. I’m making a SmartMirror at the moment.
