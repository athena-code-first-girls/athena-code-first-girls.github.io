Alexia's Story

What are you studying?

I got into my last year in school and I knew I wanted to go to University. I went through a stressful week of changing my course four times. I was juggling between Law and Medicine, and for these courses there were a lot of pre-university exams and experience I needed that I didn’t have. With Chemistry, I have always enjoyed it in school and it was one of my strongest subjects. My dad always told me to do something I enjoy and this piece of advice really sticks by me today. I am now in my 2nd year at the University of Manchester studying Chemistry.

How do you overcome a struggle?

I have waves when dealing with stress, I consider it this to a struggle. I remember applying to university was a very stressful time. Or when I needed to apply for an Australian visa. I try to not live in the future, I get little things done day by day, for example I was looking forward to being productive today.

What is your experience as woman in a STEM based course?

I’m lucky in Chemistry because the female/male ratio is equal. Out of my friends at home, only one does a STEM subject. My other friends are studious, especially my medic friends.

Who is your inspiration?

I don’t really have an inspiration, I take inspiration of confident and determined individuals - I believe it’s a good trait to have. For example, some designers such as Anna Wintour.

What is your happiest moment?

The summer before University was one my happiest times yet. In New York City, I went to the museum of modern art and enjoyed an iced matcha. Afterwards, I picked up a cheesecake walked around the streets. I have a lot of other happy moments such as being with my friends.
